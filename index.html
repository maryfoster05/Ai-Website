All About AI

This website will teach you about the relationship between Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Machine Learning (ML). It will also give a breif description of Deep Learning (DL).

Diving into NLP, ML, and DL

What NLP Does

Natural Language Processing (NLP) allows computers to understand and process human language instead of just 0s and 1s. Without NLP computers wouldn't be able to understand human languages such as English or Spanish. NLP’s capabilities include speech recognition, language translation, text summarization, and text-to-speech synthesis. The biggest way we see NLP in our daily lives is through chatbots and virtual assitants like Siri and Alexa.

What ML Does

Machine Learning (ML) allows computers to learn from data and make decisions on its own, in other words ever decision isn't precoded. ML is being used in a wide range of ways. Some include helping buisnesses understand their customers wants. This is shown by the targeted ads on Social Media apps. Another suprising thing ML does is aiding in the operating systems of self driving cars.

What DL Does

Deep Learning (DL) is a specific part of ML that teaches the computer system how to naturally seem like a human. Deep Learning is the reason why Machine Learning can help with automated driving, because it teaches the car to see stop signs and red lights like a normal human. It is able to train the computer to do this with lots of data and multiple complex algorithms.

Why This Relationship Matters

NLP and ML are two closely related fields within AI that focus on the interaction between computers and human languages. NLP provides the ability to understand and generate human language, while ML provides the ability to learn from data and make predictions or decisions. Together, NLP and ML can create powerful systems that can understand and respond to human language in ways that it’s sometimes hard to tell the difference between chatbots and humans. The combination of these two fields has led to significant progress in areas such as sentiment analysis, machine translation, natural language understanding, and dialogue systems.

NLP and ML Working Together

Sentiment Analysis
  • Determining the emotional tone of a piece of text.
  • NLP provides the ability to understand and generate human language.
  • ML provides the ability to learn from data and make predictions or decisions.
Dialogue systems
  • Computer programs that can simulate conversations with human users.
  • NLP techniques are used to extract the meaning from the text.
  • ML algorithms are used to analyze that meaning and determine the sentiment.
Chatbots
  • Computer program designed to simulate conversation with human users.
  • NLP to interpret what users say and what they intend to do.
  • ML to automatically deliver more accurate responses by learning from past interactions.
